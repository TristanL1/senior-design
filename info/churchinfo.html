Mission Church

Background Information

St. Ignatius Church is a Roman Catholic parish church named after St. Ignatius of Loyola, the founder of the Jesuits, who established the first Jesuit school in 1548. The 8th of 21 Franciscan missions established by Padre Junipero Serra, it was the first to bear the name of a woman, St. Claire of Assisi. The mission thrived at first until 1822 during Mexico's secession from the Spanish Empire, when royal funding dried up. In 1850, Bishop Joseph Alemany approached a Jesuit priest, Fr. John Nobili, S.J., to turn the parish and its lands into a college. From there, Mission Santa Clara became the first college of higher learning in the new state of California.

Constructed in the middle of the 20th century, the church was designed by architect Gerald McCue. It has a pronounced bell tower and is organized in a modernist manner with simple lines. Large stained glass windows representing scenes from St. Ignatius's life decorate the church's open and cheerful interior. The primary place of worship for the Santa Clara University community, St. Ignatius Church also functions as a parish church for locals. Daily masses are celebrated; on Sundays and holy days, there are additional services. The church also hosts many religious occasions throughout the year, such as retreats, conferences, and concerts.

One of the most notable features of St. Ignatius Church is its pipe organ, which was built in the early 20th century and is one of the largest organs on the West Coast. It is regarded as a priceless cultural asset of the Santa Clara community and has been utilized in numerous performances and recordings. Overall, St. Ignatius Church is an important spiritual and cultural landmark in Santa Clara and a cherished gathering place for both the university and the wider community.

Mass Schedule (all times in PST)

Weekday Mass

Every Monday through Friday, mass begins at 12:05 pm indoors and is open to all for in-person attendance

Sunday Masses

The Mission Church holds a morning mass at 10 am for worship, and evening masses at 5 pm and 8 pm, in which all are welcome to attend in-person. It is important to note the Sunday evening mass schedule is closely tied to SCU's academic calendar. These masses may be postponed during certain times of the year (i.e. Christmas Break, Spring Break, etc.).

Reconciliation

Reconciliation opportunities are available twice a week, on Mondays and Sundays.

  • Monday Reconciliation runs in the morning from 11:30am to 12pm when classes are in session.
  • Sunday Reconciliation is held in the evening from 7pm to 7:45pm when 8pm mass occurs.
