Louis B. Mayer Theatre

Background Information

The Mayer Theatre is a facility for the performing arts that bears the name of Paul L. Mayer, a Santa Clara graduate who was a well-known businessman and philanthropist in the Bay Area. Opening in 1977, the theater was designed by architect Paul Viemeister to have a modernist design with clear lines and a spacious, open interior. Various performing arts events, such as plays, musicals, concerts, and dance performances, are held in the theater, which can hold about 800 spectators.

The Mayer Theatre's stage features cutting-edge lighting and sound systems and a wide projection screen for multimedia presentations. The theater also has a complete set of backstage amenities, such as dressing rooms, storage spaces, and a green room. The Mayer Theatre not only acts as a performing space but also has educational uses. Acting, directing, stagecraft, and stage management are just a few of the theatre-related seminars and workshops offered by the university's Department of Theatre and Dance.
